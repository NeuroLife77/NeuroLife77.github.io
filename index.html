Dark Mode

Dominic Boutet

I am Neuroscience (major) and Computer Science (minor) student at McGill University. I am a research intern at the Baillet Lab currently working on a novel parameter space reduction approach to facilitate model calibration in high-dimensional parameter space which I originally designed for the calibration of neural mass models to resting-state MEG recordings. The overarching aim is to evaluate the ability of well calibrated neural mass models to encapsulate the necessary features within individual MEG recordings which are required to perform successful neural fingerprinting.

Beyond my current projects, I have interests the application of machine learning and artificial intelligence to neuroscience problems, along with the development of brain-inspired AI. I also have special interests in the combined use of biophysical models with machine learning techniques in the study of mental illnesses and the development of neuropharmacological treatments.
