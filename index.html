Dominic Boutet (CV)

Current work

I am a Neuroscience (Major) and Computer Science (Minor) student at McGill University. I am also a research intern at the Baillet Lab currently working on evaluating the ability of well calibrated neural mass models to encapsulate the necessary features within individual MEG recordings which are required to perform successful neural fingerprinting. Previously worked on a novel parameter space reduction approach to facilitate model calibration in high-dimensional parameter space, which I originally designed for the calibration of neural mass models to resting-state MEG recordings (preprint out soon).

Overarching research interests

Beyond my current projects, I have interests the application of machine learning and artificial intelligence to neuroscience problems, along with the development of brain-inspired AI. I also have special interests in the combined use of biophysical models with machine learning techniques in the study of mental illnesses and the development of neuropharmacological treatments.

Community involvement

I recently took the role of VP for the new machine learning & data science committee at PharmaHacks. PharmaHacks is a non-profit organization created by McGill University undergraduate students to connect university students of all levels with the pharmaceutical industry through a hackathon focused on solving life science problems with computer science solutions. I am leading a new committee that aims to help design and evaluate the problems that the participants will be solving. We are also tasked to provide resources and training in machine learning, AI, and data science to the participants.
